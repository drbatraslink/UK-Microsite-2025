What is Psoriasis?

Psoriasis is an autoimmune disease that causes a buildup of skin cells, resulting in scales forming on the skin's surface. The psoriatic scales are whitish silver and appear in dense, red patches. Psoriasis cannot be cured, but the treatment can help manage symptoms.

What are the signs and symptoms of psoriasis?

Psoriasis is a skin disease that causes a rash with itchy and scaly patches. It is most common on the knees, trunk, scalp, and elbows. The symptoms of psoriasis may vary from person to person. However, some of the most common ones are poor quality of sleep, dry and cracked skin that itches and bleeds, and patches of thick and red skin with silvery white scales that itch or burn on palms, knees, scalp, trunk, elbows, and soles of the feet.

What are the most common psoriasis triggers that cause psoriasis flare-ups?

It is essential to know your triggers, which may help you avoid a flare-up. The most common psoriasis triggers are stress, smoking, hormonal changes, especially in women, injury to your skin like an insect bite, cut, scrape or sunburn, & drinking excessive amounts of alcohol.

What is the most severe case of Psoriasis?

Erythrodermic psoriasis is the most severe form that usually affects a large skin area. It can also be triggered by a bad sunburn or taking certain medications like corticosteroids. It can lead to skin discolouration and skin shedding.

What is Eczema?

Eczema is a skin condition that causes rashes, itchiness, dry patches, and infection. It often begins with a rash, which can appear differently depending on the individual. People with lighter skin tones can have a rash that looks purple, red, or pink, while those with darker skin can have a rash that looks brown, purple, or grey.

How can you treat Eczema?

There is no cure for eczema, but it can be managed with treatments such as moisturisers, corticosteroids, phototherapy, antihistamines, and immunosuppressants. It is advised to take expert consultation before starting any medications.

Does Eczema spread through touch?

Eczema can spread to different parts of a person's body but not from contact. It does not spread from person to person. It is best to use the treatment and prevention methods suggested by the healthcare provider or doctor to help prevent eczema from spreading.

What causes Eczema to flare up?

Various factors can cause eczema to flare up. These include stress, cold weather, and allergens. However, for people suffering from other kinds of eczema, finding the cause of the flare- up is difficult.

What is vitiligo?

Vitiligo is a skin condition that causes the skin to lose its colour or pigmentation. The white patches appear symmetrically on both sides of the body, including both hands and knees. People with vitiligo can also develop other autoimmune disorders.

What triggers vitiligo to spread?

Vitiligo can spread due to reasons including stress, sunburn, chemical exposure, and skin damage. This skin condition is progressive, and there is no way to determine how quickly it will spread or how much skin pigmentation you will lose.

Can Vitiligo stop suddenly?

There is no cure for Vitiligo. However, treatment can stop or slow the discolouring process and return some colour to a person's skin.

What precautions should be taken if one suffers from Vitiligo?

If a person is suffering from Vitiligo, they should follow self-care tactics like avoiding tattoos, protecting the skin from sun and UV light, and using water-resistant sunscreen with an SPF of 30.

What is alopecia?

Alopecia is a loss or absence of hair from an area where it usually grows. It can affect your scalp and entire body and be temporary or permanent.

Which alopecia is reversible?

Alopecia areata is a condition that can be reversed. Some patients experience spontaneous new hair growth without requiring further treatment. Some individuals experience an improvement in their condition following corticosteroid treatment, steroid injections, or other therapeutic options.

How do I know if my alopecia is growing?

There are cases where single or multiple bald patches may develop a few weeks after the first. Only some others will experience regrowth on some patches and see new patches develop across other areas of the scalp. Also, in some cases, small patches may merge to form a large bald patch.

Is Alopecia transmitted?

Alopecia is not contagious. People suffering from alopecia areata have some emotional triggers like physical and emotional stress and family history.
